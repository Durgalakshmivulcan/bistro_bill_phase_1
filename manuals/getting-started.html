Getting Started with Bistro Bill

Welcome to Bistro Bill, your comprehensive restaurant management and point-of-sale system. This guide will help you understand the basic layout, navigation, and core concepts of the application.

System Overview

Bistro Bill is a web-based restaurant management system that combines point-of-sale operations, inventory management, customer relationship management, and business analytics into one integrated platform.

Key Features

  • Point of Sale (POS): Take orders, manage tables, process payments
  • Catalog Management: Manage products, menus, and pricing across different sales channels
  • Inventory Tracking: Monitor stock levels, create purchase orders, track suppliers
  • Customer Management: Build customer profiles, loyalty programs, and track order history
  • Marketing Tools: Create discounts, promotions, and collect customer feedback
  • Analytics & Reports: Sales reports, GST reports, audit logs
  • Business Settings: Configure branches, taxes, payment methods, and operating hours

Login and Role-Based Access

Bistro Bill uses role-based access control, meaning what you see and can do depends on your assigned role:

User Roles

  • Cashier: Access to POS, order taking, table management, and customer information
  • Manager: All cashier features plus catalog, inventory, purchase orders, marketing, and reports
  • Owner: All manager features plus business settings, branch management, and full system configuration
  • Super Admin: Platform-wide administration including business owner management and system settings

When you log in, you'll be directed to your default dashboard based on your role. Your username and role are displayed in the top-right corner of the screen.

Understanding Dashboard Layout

Most features in Bistro Bill use the Dashboard Layout, which provides consistent navigation and structure across the application.

Dashboard Layout Components

1. Sidebar Navigation

Located on the left side of the screen (can be toggled on mobile). The sidebar contains links to all major modules:

  • Business Owner Dashboard
  • Point of Sale
  • All Orders
  • Reservations
  • Catalog
  • Inventory
  • Purchase Order
  • Customers
  • Marketing
  • Analytics & Reports
  • Manage Resources
  • Business Settings

Note: You'll only see menu items your role has permission to access.

2. Top Bar

Located at the top of every page, the top bar includes:

  • Menu Toggle: Hamburger icon (on mobile) to show/hide sidebar
  • Page Title: Shows the current page or module name
  • Search Bar: Global search functionality (centered on desktop)
  • Notifications Bell: View system notifications and alerts
  • Profile Menu: Access to My Account, Manage Password, and Logout

3. Main Content Area

The central area where page content, forms, tables, and data are displayed. This area updates when you navigate to different pages while the sidebar and top bar remain constant.

Understanding POS Layout

The Point of Sale (POS) Layout is a specialized interface designed for taking orders and managing restaurant operations. It has a different structure from the Dashboard Layout to optimize the order-taking workflow.

POS vs Dashboard Layout

Feature Dashboard Layout POS Layout
Navigation Full sidebar with all modules Simplified, focused on POS operations
Use Case Configuration, reporting, management Order taking, payment processing
Typical Users Managers, Owners, Admins Cashiers, Servers, Kitchen Staff
Pages Included Dashboard, Catalog, Inventory, Reports, Settings Take Order, My Orders, Table View, Reservations

Tip: You can quickly switch between POS and Dashboard views using the sidebar navigation. The system remembers your last location in each layout.

Navigation Patterns

Understanding how to navigate efficiently in Bistro Bill will help you work faster and find what you need quickly.

Common Navigation Actions

  • Accessing Modules: Click any item in the sidebar to navigate to that module
  • Breadcrumbs: Many pages show breadcrumb navigation at the top to track your location
  • Back to Dashboard: Click the Bistro Bill logo in the sidebar to return to your main dashboard
  • Quick Search: Use the search bar in the top bar to find products, customers, or orders
  • Action Buttons: Look for "Add New," "Edit," "Delete," and other action buttons typically located at the top-right of list pages

Standard Page Layouts

Most feature pages in Bistro Bill follow consistent patterns:

  • List/Dashboard Pages: Show tables or cards with data, filters, search, and action buttons
  • Add/Create Pages: Forms with required fields, validation, and Save/Cancel buttons
  • Edit Pages: Pre-filled forms with the ability to update and save changes
  • View/Details Pages: Read-only display of complete record information with Edit and Delete options

Mobile and Tablet Access

Bistro Bill is fully responsive and works on tablets and mobile devices. On smaller screens:

  • The sidebar is hidden by default - tap the menu icon (☰) in the top-left to open it
  • Tables may scroll horizontally or stack vertically for easier viewing
  • Action buttons may be grouped into dropdown menus
  • The search bar may be hidden in the top bar - access it via a search icon

Notifications and Alerts

Bistro Bill keeps you informed about important events through the notification system:

  • Click the bell icon in the top bar to view notifications
  • Notifications include purchase order updates, approvals, system alerts, and more
  • Each notification shows the date and can be dismissed individually

Quick Tips for New Users

💡 Use Search

The global search bar helps you quickly find products, customers, or orders without navigating through menus.

💡 Watch for Active Indicators

The current page is highlighted in the sidebar with a yellow background (matching Bistro Bill's brand color).

💡 Check Required Fields

When filling out forms, fields marked with an asterisk (*) are required before you can save.

💡 Confirm Before Deleting

The system will always ask for confirmation before permanently deleting records to prevent accidental data loss.
